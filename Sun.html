Sun

The Sun is the primary source of energy for life on Earth. It is the closest star to the Earth, about 150 million kilometers from Earth.
The temperature of the Sun’s visible surface (photosphere) is about 6000 degrees Celsius.
However, the outer layer of the Sun’s atmosphere, known as the Corona, is, on average, about 2 million degrees Celsius.
The core of the Sun is the center and is about 15 million degrees Celsius.

The Sun is made up of 73% hydrogen and 25% helium. It also has trace amounts of oxygen, carbon, iron, and other elements.
It is classified as a G-Type Main Sequence Star. It is about 4.6 billion years old and will continue to shine for another 5 billion years.
After that, it will grow into a Red Giant and then finally end its life as a white dwarf.
